## Caption Framework

### Alligator Wisdom (2-3 posts per week)
**Focus:** Legal insights, thought leadership, educational content

```
[Opening hook - statement or question that catches attention]

[Context - why this matters / real-world example]

[Explanation - what you should know]

[Action - what to do with this information]

[CTA - invite engagement, not sales]
```

**Example:**
```
Most motorcycle accident victims settle too fast.

I see it constantly: Someone gets injured, insurance makes an offer within weeks, 
they take it. Case closed.

The problem? They don't know if that offer is fair. They don't know their long-term 
medical costs. They don't understand what their case is actually worth.

Here's what I tell my clients: Don't rush. Get proper medical evaluation first. 
Understand your injuries—surgery, ongoing therapy, permanent damage? That costs money.

Then—and only then—negotiate. A good attorney can often get 2-3x what insurance 
initially offered.

What questions do you have about motorcycle accident claims?

#MotorcycleAccidents #PersonalInjury #TrialLaw #LegalAdvice
```

**Length:** 150-300 words (LinkedIn favors longer-form)  
**Tone:** Professional but honest, educational not salesy  
**Structure:** Problem → Context → Solution → CTA  
**CTA:** Question or invitation (not "call me now")

## Article Framework (1 per month)

**Long-form articles on LinkedIn** drive authority and reach.

### Structure
```
Headline: [Strong, specific, benefit-driven]

Introduction (2-3 paragraphs):
- Hook the reader
- Explain why this matters
- Preview what they'll learn

Body (3-5 sections):
- Section 1: Problem statement
- Section 2: Common mistakes
- Section 3: How to do it right
- Section 4: Real example or case study
- Section 5: Actionable takeaway

Conclusion:
- Summary
- Call to further engagement
- Optional: link to firm website
```

### Article Ideas
- "5 Mistakes Motorcycle Accident Victims Make"
- "Understanding Your Motorcycle Insurance in NC"
- "Why Trial Experience Matters"
- "The Cost of Quick Settlements"
- "What Every Injured Rider Should Know"

### Article Length
- Optimal: 800-1500 words
- Minimum: 500 words
- Structure with headers for readability
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)

### Article Publishing
- Post directly to LinkedIn (native articles)
- Or reference external blog post
- Promote with social posts (link in comments)

## Carousel/Document Posts

**Format for multi-slide educational content:**

```
Slide 1: [Headline / question]
Slide 2: [Point 1]
Slide 3: [Point 2]
Slide 4: [Point 3]
Slide 5: [Takeaway / CTA]
```

**Example: "5 Questions After a Motorcycle Accident"**

```
Slide 1: "Just had a motorcycle accident? Ask yourself these 5 questions."

Slide 2: "1. Am I injured? (Even if you feel okay now, some injuries show up later. 
Get medical eval.)"

Slide 3: "2. Who was at fault? (Get names, contact info, insurance info of other party. 
Don't admit fault.)"

Slide 4: "3. Do I have witnesses? (Get contact info. Eyewitnesses matter.)"

Slide 5: "4. Do I have photos? (Document the scene, vehicle damage, your injuries.)"

Slide 6: "5. Do I have a lawyer? (Before talking to insurance. Seriously.)"

Don't rush. Get these details right and you'll be in much better position.

Questions? I'm happy to help.
```

## Engagement Protocol

**After posting:**
1. Respond to all comments within 24 hours
2. Reply thoughtfully (not just "thanks!")
3. Engage with others' posts in your network
4. Share others' relevant content
5. Connect with people who engage consistently

**Tone:** Professional but warm, knowledgeable but not arrogant

## Hashtag Strategy (LinkedIn-specific)

**LinkedIn hashtags are less critical than Instagram**, but helpful:
- 3-5 hashtags acceptable (less spammy than Instagram)
- Focus on professional topics
- Examples: #TrialLaw #PersonalInjury #LegalProfessional #MotorcycleSafety

**Hashtags to avoid:**
- Overly trendy (#Amazing #Blessed)
- Too many (#Hash #Tag #Spam)
- Irrelevant to content

## Voice Guidelines for LinkedIn

**LinkedIn voice = Professional + Human**

✅ DO:
- Be authoritative (you know your stuff)
- Be honest (real experiences, real failures)
- Be helpful (people first, agenda second)
- Be clear (no jargon without explanation)
- Share insights (what have you learned?)

❌ DON'T:
- Oversell your services
- Use corporate speak
- Be overly casual (this is professional network)
- Preach or lecture
- Make everything about you (be generous with others' content)
