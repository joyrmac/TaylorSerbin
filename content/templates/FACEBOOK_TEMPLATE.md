## Caption Framework

### Rider's Life Stories (Facebook can be longer-form)
```
[Hook or opening moment]

[Detailed story or experience]

[Reflection or connection to values]

[Optional: Question for community or CTA]

[Tags: @ local motorcycle groups, shops, venues if relevant]
```

**Example:**
```
Took a bunch of riders up to the Blue Ridge today.

Some of them were nervous—first big group ride. But there's something about that 
community that just works. No egos. Nobody trying to prove anything. Just people 
who love the road and want to share that with each other.

By the end of the day, those nervous riders? They were laughing, swapping numbers, 
planning the next ride. That's what motorcycle culture really is. Not fast bikes 
or fancy gear. It's that moment when strangers become a crew.

Ride safe out there. And if you're thinking about joining a group—do it. 
The worst that happens is you make some friends.

Who's riding this weekend? 🏍️

#NCRiders #MotorcycleCommunity #BlueRidge #RideLife
```

**Length:** 150-300 words (Facebook allows longer, use it for stories)  
**Tone:** Community-focused, warm, welcoming  
**CTA:** Invitation or question, not sales  
**Tags:** Tag relevant people/places/groups  

---

### Cooking & Entertaining (Longer storytelling)
```
[Dish or entertaining moment]

[Story of the prep/experience]

[Why this matters / what it brings]

[Optional: Recipe teaser or invitation]
```

**Example:**
```
Hosted dinner last weekend. Nothing fancy—just good food and good people.

Made escargot, fresh pasta, and a simple green salad. The whole prep took about 
3 hours, but that's not why people come. They come for the moment.

There's something about sitting around a table, working through multiple courses, 
slowing down and actually talking to each other. No rush. No agenda. Just dinner.

That's the whole point of cooking. Not to impress with fancy techniques (though 
technique matters). It's to create that moment where people feel like they matter.

If you want the escargot recipe, I'll post it next week. But honestly? It's just 
butter, garlic, and good snails. The rest is just knowing when to stop overthinking 
and serve it.

Who wants to come to the next one? 🍷

#GourmetCooking #Entertaining #FrenchCuisine #FoodCulture
```

**Length:** 150-250 words  
**Tone:** Warm, inviting, reflective  
**Focus:** The experience, not just the food  
**CTA:** Invitation to join

### Community Announcements
```
[Event name and details]

[Why you're sponsoring/attending/excited about it]

[How to participate]

[Event details: Date, time, location, link]
```

**Example:**
```
Sponsoring the Carolina Riders Toy Run this Saturday.

If you haven't done this before: It's the coolest Saturday of the year. Hundreds 
of riders, toys loaded on sidecars, headed to the children's hospital. The kids' 
faces when the riders show up? Worth every cold morning.

If you're riding: Bring a toy. New, unwrapped, something a kid would actually want. 
Meet at the Shell station on Main at 8am sharp.

If you can't ride but want to help: Come to the hospital at 10:30am. Help unload. 
Say hi to the riders. Be part of it.

This is community. This is why I ride.

CAROLINA RIDERS TOY RUN
Saturday, December 15th
8:00 AM - Shell Station on Main
Toys for Children's Hospital

See you there. 🎁

#ToyRun #CarolinaRiders #MotorcycleCommunity #CommunityGiving
```

**Length:** 150-200 words  
**Tone:** Enthusiastic, community-focused  
**Details:** Clear date, time, location, action steps  
**CTA:** RSVP or participation invitation

### Client Testimonials / Case Wins
```
[Client story (anonymized)]

[What was at stake]

[How we helped]

[Outcome]

[Optional: Quote from client]
```

**Example:**
```
Had a case wrap up last month. Can't share details, but I can tell you this:

Client came to us after an accident. Insurance offered $50k. He wasn't sure it was 
enough. Turned out it wasn't—medical costs were higher than anyone initially thought.

We pushed back. Got discovery. Found some stuff the adjuster missed. Took it to trial.

Final verdict: $180k. More than 3x the initial offer.

That's the difference between accepting what sounds like a lot and actually 
understanding what your case is worth.

If you've been in an accident, don't settle without talking to someone who knows 
the real value of your case.

#TrialWins #PersonalInjury #JusticeMatters #NotSettling
```

**Length:** 100-150 words  
**Tone:** Professional, matter-of-fact, proud  
**Privacy:** Anonymize client details  
**CTA:** Invitation to reach out (not pushy)

## Longer-Form Stories

Facebook is perfect for longer storytelling (500-1000 words). Use this for:
- Detailed case stories (anonymized)
- Community history or connections
- Personal growth or lessons learned
- Riding adventures
- Cooking philosophy

### Structure:
1. **Opening:** Hook them in first sentence
2. **Context:** Why this story matters
3. **Story:** Tell it with detail
4. **Lesson:** What did you learn?
5. **Connection:** How does this serve others?
6. **CTA:** What do you want readers to do?

## Video Posts

**Facebook video best practices:**
- Vertical or square (9:16 or 1:1)
- Under 2 minutes ideal
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Captions (many watch muted)
- Authentic over polished

**Ideas:**
- Short clips from events
- Cooking demonstrations
- Riding moments
- Testimonial snippets
- Quick legal tips

---

## Image Requirements

### Photo Posts
- Minimum: 1080x1080px
- Optimal: 1200x1200px or larger
- Format: JPEG or PNG
- Quality: High quality, authentic feeling

### Carousel Posts
- Each slide: 1200x1200px (square) or 1080x1350px (vertical)
- Consistent aspect ratio
- 2-10 slides typically

### Video Posts
- Resolution: 1080p minimum
- Format: MP4 or MOV
- Duration: Under 2 minutes optimal
- Vertical 9:16 or square 1:1 optimal

## Engagement & Community Management

**Facebook algorithm favors engagement:**
- Respond to comments within 24 hours
- Reply warmly and authentically (as Jason)
- Ask questions that invite discussion

- Use Facebook features (polls, questions, etc.)

**Community guidelines:**
- Be welcoming to new people
- Answer questions thoroughly
- Don't delete critical comments (respond instead)
- Keep community standards clear and enforced
- Celebrate community wins
