## Core Competency Requirements

Before joining any team, members must demonstrate:

### 1. Understanding of the Doctrine
- Can articulate the Operating Manual's core principles without references
- Makes decisions that align with "clarity beats cleverness"
- Understands why restraint matters more than volume
- Never conflicts with the North Star

**Assessment:** Can you explain why the firm would reject a "clever" campaign in favor of a clear one?

### 2. Brand Authority Comprehension
- Understands the two-brand system (personal + corporate)
- Can recognize authentic vs. manufactured content
- Knows the "Alligator Logo Test" and applies it
- Respects the brand guardrails without question

**Assessment:** Can you explain why Jason's personal brand is authentic and how that serves the firm?

### 3. Communication Discipline
- Writes clearly and directly (no fluff, no hype)
- Understands tone variation across platforms
- Can match Jason's voice (gritty, direct, expert but accessible)
- Follows execution standards consistently

**Assessment:** Can you write a social post caption that sounds like Jason without sounding corporate?

## Cross-Functional Skills

### Everyone Should Have
- **Doctrine mastery:** Can cite the Operating Manual from memory
- **Communication clarity:** Says what you mean, no corporate speak
- **Documentation discipline:** Updates the repo when things change
- **Flexibility within constraint:** Works creatively within doctrine guardrails
- **Long-term thinking:** Makes decisions that serve the brand in 12 months, not today

### Escalation Judgment
Knowing when to ask vs. when to decide:

- **Routine decisions** → Social Manager makes the call
- **Tone/voice questions** → Reviewer approves
- **Legal/accuracy questions** → Firm Contact reviews
- **Strategy questions** → Reference the manual, escalate if unclear
- **Doctrine violations** → Always escalate, never override

## Assessment & Development

### Onboarding Checklist
New team members must demonstrate:
- [ ] Can explain the Operating Manual
- [ ] Can identify authentic vs. manufactured content
- [ ] Can write in Jason's voice
- [ ] Understands the two-brand system
- [ ] Knows their role's specific responsibilities
- [ ] Can navigate the repository
- [ ] Understands escalation path

**Recommended:** Shadow current team member for 2 weeks before independent work.

### Ongoing Development
- **Monthly:** Review performance against doctrine
- **Quarterly:** Strategy deep-dive and skill assessment
- **As-needed:** Feedback on specific posts or decisions

### Skill Improvement Areas

**If someone struggles with tone/voice:**
- Re-read EXECUTION_STANDARDS.md and BRANDING_GUIDE.md
- Study 5–10 past posts that hit the tone well
- Write captions in pairs with someone who has the skill
- Get detailed feedback on what's off

**If someone struggles with doctrine application:**
- Practice the decision framework (what level of doctrine applies?)
- Review the Operating Manual weekly (highlight key sections)
- Discuss decisions before publishing
- Ask "What does the North Star say about this?" for every call

**If someone struggles with platform mechanics:**
- Take platform certification courses (Meta Blueprint, LinkedIn Learning)
- Study trending content in comparable niches
- Practice with non-critical posts first
- Build a testing schedule for algorithm changes

## Expectations & Culture

### We Value
- **Clarity:** Say what you mean. No corporate speak, no marketing fluff.
- **Authenticity:** Every person on this team cares about the brand's authenticity.
- **Restraint:** We choose not to over-publish, not to hype, not to chase trends.
- **Authority:** We're confident in what we know. We say "I don't know" when we don't.
- **Learning:** We read the doctrine regularly. We discuss decisions. We improve.

### We Don't Value
- Vanity metrics (follower count, impressions, likes)
- Trend-chasing (posting because something's trending)
- Volume over quality
- Corporate jargon
- Manufactured authenticity

### Decision-Making Culture
- Cite the manual first
- Propose solutions, not just problems
- Document decisions in git commits
- Disagree and commit (once a decision is made, everyone supports it)
- Push back on doctrine violations (that's your job)
