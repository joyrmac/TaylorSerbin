Design Guidelines

Photography Standards

  • High-quality: Professional camera or high-end phone camera. Good lighting.
  • Raw aesthetic: Not overly filtered or polished. Real office lighting, real road conditions, real kitchen vibes.
  • Action over pose: Capture Jason doing things, not standing for the camera.
  • In-the-moment: Authentic moments from real rides, real cooking, real events.
  • Real backgrounds: Actual roads, actual kitchen, actual shooting range—not studio setup.

Color Palette (For All Templates)

  • Primary Green #006838 — Accent bars, highlights, logo color
  • Dark Green #01321D — Primary background, overlays, serious content
  • Black #000000 — Text, depth, contrast
  • White #FFFFFF — Text on dark, breathing room
  • NO bright yellow. Save any accent color for only critical CTAs (phone number when needed).

Typography

  • Headlines: Nunito Sans 900 (Bold), 24-32pt, white or light green text on dark
  • Body text: Nunito Sans 400 (Regular), 14-16pt, white on dark overlay
  • Minimal text on image: Max 1-2 short lines. Let photo breathe.
  • Captions in post: 2-4 sentences. Direct, conversational, Jason's voice.

Alligator Logo Usage

  • Placement: Top right corner (subtle) or watermark at 6-8% opacity across background
  • Purpose: Marks this as "The Litigator" brand. Distinct from firm branding.
  • Visibility: Should be visible but not dominating. It's a brand mark, not a decoration.
  • Consistency: Use same logo size/placement across template type.

Text Overlay Rules

  • Dark overlays required: 60-80% opacity dark green or black at bottom of photo-based posts (ensures text readability)
  • Accent bar always: 4px green bar for visual anchor (left side or top)
  • Keep it short: One headline, max 2-3 lines. Caption does the storytelling.
  • White text on dark: Always. Black text on light backgrounds only for text-only templates.
  • Mobile first: All text readable on 4-inch phone screen. No tiny fonts.

Caption Strategy (The Heavy Lifting)

  • Captions are where the story lives. Image sets mood, caption tells the truth.
  • Jason's voice: Direct, honest, conversational. Like talking over a cigar.
  • 2-4 sentences max. Mobile users scrolling fast. Grab attention, tell truth, move on.
  • Connect to values: Rider safety tips → "I've seen accidents." Cooking → "This is how I do it." Shooting → "Same discipline as trial."
  • No corporate speak. "Just rolled in from a ride" not "Completed motorcycle excursion experience"

What Makes These Templates "Gritty"

  • Raw photography: Not Instagram-filtered. Real lighting, real shadows, real imperfection.
  • Minimal design: Text gets out of the way. Photo and caption do the work.
  • Dark aesthetic: Dark green and black dominate. Serious, protective, trustworthy.
  • No fluff: No rounded corners, no gradients, no decorative elements. Professional and direct.
  • Alligator featured: This marks it as Jason's brand, distinct from corporate.
  • Authentic moments: Real action, not posed. Real life documented.

Common Template Sizes

  • Instagram Feed Post: 1080x1350 (vertical)
  • Instagram Story/Reel: 1080x1920 (full vertical)
  • Facebook Post: 1200x1500 (vertical) or 1200x628 (horizontal)
  • LinkedIn Post: 1200x627 (horizontal)

Brand Voice Checklist

  • ✅ Authentic: Real moment from real life
  • ✅ Gritty: Raw, unpolished, high-quality
  • ✅ Direct: Straight talk, no corporate jargon
  • ✅ Expert but accessible: Jason knows his stuff, shares honestly
  • ✅ Alligator branded: Logo visible, distinctive, marks this as personal brand
  • ✅ Protective: "I'm looking out for you" energy
  • ✅ Community: Member of motorcycle/lifestyle communities, not marketing to them
  • ❌ NOT: Corporate polish, manufactured moments, fake lifestyle, hard-sell legal
