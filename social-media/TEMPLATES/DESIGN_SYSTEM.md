# Taylor | Serbin — Social Media Design System

**Updated:** May 21, 2026  
**Purpose:** Visual design templates and brand consistency for all social platforms  
**Designed for:** Authentic, professional-but-gritty positioning for blue-collar audience

---

## Design Philosophy

### What NOT to Do
❌ Corporate sterile (boring, doesn't fit)  
❌ Stock photo polish (fake, untrustworthy)  
❌ Overly designed (manufactured, not authentic)  
❌ Generic lawyer branding (looks like 10,000 other firms)  
❌ Too much color/effects (distracting, unprofessional)

### What TO Do
✅ Raw, authentic photography (real moments, real people)  
✅ High-quality production (professional, not cheap)  
✅ Strategic use of color (green accent, yellow CTA, lots of white space)  
✅ Bold typography (statements, not whispers)  
✅ The Litigator branding (alligator logo is a STATEMENT)  
✅ Text hierarchy (headlines matter, captions matter)  
✅ Responsive mobile design (90% viewing on phone)

## Color System

### Primary Brand Colors

**Primary Green: #006838**
- Used for: Accents, borders, highlights, CTA buttons
- Personality: Trustworthy, strong, legal authority
- Usage: Sparingly—2-3% of post

**Dark Green: #01321D**
- Used for: Text, dark accents, shadows
- Personality: Depth, grounding, seriousness
- Usage: Headlines, important text

**CTA Yellow: #F6C01A**
- Used for: Call-to-action buttons, phone numbers, critical info
- Personality: Attention-grabbing, warmth, action
- Usage: Reserve for CTAs only (phone, "click here", forms)

**White: #FFFFFF**
- Used for: Background, negative space, breathing room
- Personality: Clean, approachable, clarity
- Usage: Majority of design

**Black Text: #000000**
- Used for: Body text, captions, detail copy
- Personality: Professional, readable, authoritative
- Usage: All readable text

**Accent Gray: #666666**
- Used for: Secondary text, borders, subtle hierarchy
- Personality: Supporting role, less important
- Usage: Dates, metadata, tertiary information

## Typography

### Headline Font: Nunito Sans Bold (900 weight)
- Used for: Main headlines, practice areas, major claims
- Size: 32-48pt (Instagram), 24-32pt (Stories/Reels)
- Color: Dark Green (#01321D) or Black
- Style: ALL CAPS or Title Case (never sentence case)
- Example: "MOTORCYCLE ACCIDENT?" or "Jason E. Taylor"
- Personality: Bold, confident, no-nonsense

### Subheading Font: Nunito Sans Semi-Bold (700 weight)
- Used for: Secondary information, attorney names, practice area callouts
- Size: 18-24pt
- Color: Dark Green (#01321D) or Primary Green
- Style: Title Case
- Example: "Chief Trial Litigator" or "Workers' Compensation"
- Personality: Strong but supporting

### Body Text Font: Nunito Sans Regular (400 weight)
- Used for: Captions, educational content, case descriptions
- Size: 14-18pt (on-image), 16pt (in caption)
- Color: Black (#000000)
- Style: Sentence case, natural language
- Example: Caption text, longer explanations
- Personality: Accessible, readable, conversational

### Caption Font (Social): Nunito Sans Regular (400 weight)
- Used for: Instagram captions, Facebook post text
- Size: 16pt (displayed, but readable on phone)
- Color: Black
- Style: Natural, direct, Jason's voice
- Personality: Authentic, not overly formal

## Mobile Optimization (CRITICAL)

### All designs must be readable on 4" phone screen:

✅ Headline: 24pt+ (readable without zoom)  
✅ Body text: 14pt+ (comfortable reading)  
✅ Text contrast: 4.5:1 ratio minimum (readability)  
✅ CTA button: 44x44px minimum (tap-able)  
✅ White space: Generous margins (breathing room)  
✅ Color overlay: Dark enough for text readability  
✅ Single column: Not side-by-side on vertical posts  

### Instagram Reels/Stories Specific:
- Full vertical format (1080x1920)
- 5-7 second clips (short, punchy)
- Text in center (where people tap)
- Hook in first 1 second (attention)
- CTA on last frame (call to action)

## Consistency Rules (Brand Guard)

### For ALL Posts

✅ **Color Usage:**
- Yellow = CTA only (phone numbers, buttons, "click here")
- Green = Accents, borders, secondary (not overwhelming)
- White = Primary background
- Black = Text and depth

✅ **Typography:**
- Nunito Sans family only (no exceptions)
- Headline = Bold/Extra Bold
- Body = Regular weight
- No script fonts, no serifs, no decorative fonts

✅ **Photography:**
- High-quality, professional camera or phone with good light
- No blurry images
- Real moments > posed moments
- Good composition (rule of thirds, clear subject)
- No excessive filters (slight enhancement OK, not cartoon-y)

✅ **Logo Placement:**
- Firm logo: Bottom right or bottom left, small (not intrusive)
- Alligator logo: Prominent for Jason brand, branded legal tips
- Both should be watermarked (copyright protection)

✅ **Text Hierarchy:**
- One headline per post (clear focal point)
- Maximum 2 font sizes on image (headline + body)
- Captions are king (write good ones)
- Use white space between text blocks

### For FIRM Brand Posts (Approach A)

- Use green accent bar (left border or top bar)
- Include yellow CTA button
- Professional tone throughout
- Alligator logo present but not dominant
- Phone number prominent and yellow-highlighted

### For JASON Personal Brand Posts (Approach B)

- Alligator logo is FEATURED (not hidden)
- Raw, authentic photography
- Minimal text on image
- Direct, conversational caption tone
- Photo speaks, caption explains

## Next Steps

1. **Choose design tool:**
   - Canva (easiest, fastest, templates available)
   - Adobe XD (more control, steeper learning curve)
   - Figma (collaborative, cloud-based)
   - Hire a designer (if budget allows)

2. **Build the 10 master templates**
   - Start with 3 most-used templates
   - Test them with real content
   - Iterate based on what works

3. **Create asset library:**
   - Organize in Google Drive or design tool
   - Label clearly (lawyers hate searching)
   - Version control (v1, v2, final, etc.)
   - Share with anyone who might edit

4. **Document usage:**
   - When to use each template
   - How to customize (what can change, what can't)
   - Who can edit (designers only? or anyone?)
   - Brand guard rules (enforce consistency)
