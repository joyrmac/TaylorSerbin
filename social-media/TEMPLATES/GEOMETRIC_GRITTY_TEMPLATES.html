Geometric Design Rules

The Core Principle

  • Real photos + designed overlays. Not filtered. Designed. These are two separate things layered intentionally.
  • Geometric patterns are VISIBLE. Diagonal lines, grids, shapes—they're part of the design, not hidden.
  • Dark overlays are the base. Then patterns layer on top. Then accents layer on top of that.
  • Photo quality matters. But design sophistication matters MORE. Design IS the brand marker.

Pattern Overlays (The Design)

  • Diagonal Lines: 45° angle stripes (20-24px spacing). 10-15% opacity. Creates motion and energy.
  • Grid Pattern: Intersecting horizontal + vertical lines (30px spacing). 10% opacity. Creates structure and precision.
  • Combined Patterns: Diagonal + grid together = sophisticated complexity. Use on hero shots.
  • Stripe Accents: Solid diagonal stripes (6-8px wide). Full opacity. Used as design highlights.
  • All patterns in green (#006838). Visible but not overwhelming. They frame the photo.

Geometric Accent Shapes

  • Vertical lines: 3-6px wide, 30-50% of image height. Placed top right or left edge.
  • Horizontal lines: 3-6px high, 40-60% of image width. Top or bottom placement.
  • Corner brackets: L-shaped corners (40x40px minimum). 3px stroke. Top left or bottom right.
  • Diagonal stripes: 45° angle, 6-8px wide. Placed as accents (bottom/right typically).
  • All accents in bright green (#006838). Sharp, visible, not soft.

Color System (Geometric Edition)

  • Bright Green #006838 — All geometric shapes, lines, accents. Sharp. Visible.
  • Dark Green #01321D — Background overlays, gradients. Base color.
  • Black #000000 — Primary overlay color. Depth. Contrast.
  • White #FFFFFF — Text on dark. Headlines and body.
  • No other colors. This is disciplined. Green/black/white only.

Overlay Layering (The Stack)

  • Layer 1 — Photo: Real image (motorcycle, food, action)
  • Layer 2 — Dark Overlay: 60-75% opacity black/dark green gradient
  • Layer 3 — Pattern(s): Diagonal lines and/or grid (10-15% opacity)
  • Layer 4 — Geometric Accents: Lines, corners, stripes (full opacity, green)
  • Layer 5 — Content: Text with dark gradient background at bottom

Typography (With Design)

  • Headlines: Nunito Sans 900 weight. 36-44pt. ALL CAPS. Letter-spacing -1.5px. White text.
  • Accent words: Same size but color: #006838. Creates visual hierarchy within headline.
  • Body text: Nunito Sans 400 weight. 14-16pt. White on dark. Line height 1.6+.
  • Minimal text on image. Max 1-2 lines. Captions do heavy lifting.
  • Sharp, aggressive typography. Not friendly. Bold and direct.

Alligator Logo Placement

  • Standard Placement: Top right, 25% opacity. Large (60-80px).
  • Watermark Style: Center-background, 8-10% opacity. Huge (120-150px).
  • Consistency: Same placement per template type across all posts.
  • Never hide it. It's THE brand mark. Jason's personal brand identifier.

What Makes This "Geometric Gritty"

  • ✅ Designed, not filtered: Intentional graphic elements, not auto-generated
  • ✅ Patterns visible: Diagonal lines and grids are seen, not hidden
  • ✅ Multiple layer stack: Photo → overlay → patterns → accents → text (sophisticated)
  • ✅ Geometric shapes: Lines, corners, stripes used intentionally as design
  • ✅ Bright green accents: Sharp, not soft. Marks it as Jason's brand.
  • ✅ Dark base: 60-75% dark overlay creates seriousness and edge
  • ✅ Real photos + design work: Two separate, intentional layers
  • ✅ Modern aesthetic: Sophisticated design + street-level edge. Not corporate. Not Instagram.
  • ❌ NOT: Filters, soft gradients, rounded corners, corporate polish, single-layer design

Caption Strategy

  • Image + design set the mood. Caption tells the story and context.
  • 2-4 sentences max. Direct. Jason's voice. Peer-level tone.
  • Example: "Just rolled in from 200 miles through the mountains. Clean roads, good weather, better company. If you're riding tomorrow, check your brakes first. Physics doesn't negotiate with you."
  • No corporate speak. No marketing language. Real person, real moment.

Image Specifications

  • Instagram Feed: 1080x1350px (vertical). All geometric patterns same.
  • Instagram Story/Reel: 1080x1920px (full vertical). Scale patterns accordingly.
  • Facebook: 1200x1500px (vertical) or 1200x628px (horizontal).
  • DPI: 72 DPI for web.
  • Pattern consistency: Same diagonal spacing, same grid size across all sizes.
