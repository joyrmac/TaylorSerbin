# Google Search Console Performance Analysis

**Updated:** May 21, 2026  
**Data Source:** GSC data provided (location page CTR 0.01-0.35%, blog content CTR 1.5-3.7%+)  
**Critical Finding:** Location pages significantly underperforming vs. blog/resource content

---

## Key Finding: The Performance Gap

### Location Pages (Geographic Pages)

- **CTR Range:** 0.01% - 0.35%
- **Status:** ⚠️ **CRITICALLY UNDERPERFORMING**
- **Problem:** Despite being in Tier 4 of the page rotation priority, location pages are driving virtually no traffic from organic search

### Blog & Resource Content

- **CTR Range:** 1.5% - 3.7%+
- **Status:** ✅ **STRONG PERFORMANCE**
- **Problem:** This is working well but is being deprioritized in current rotation strategy

---

## What This Means

### The Contradiction

The **GEOGRAPHIC_STRATEGY_UPDATED.md** recommends:
- Rotating location pages (Weeks 27-36 of 50-week calendar)
- Heavy investment in location page optimization
- Geographic targeting as major traffic driver

**But GSC data shows:**
- Location pages are near-invisible in organic search performance
- Blog content is the real traffic driver (10x+ better performance)
- Location page optimization effort may be misaligned

### Why This Matters

1. **Content Calendar Imbalance:** Dedicating 10 weeks (27-36) to location pages when they drive 0.01-0.35% CTR is inefficient
2. **SEO Resource Mismatch:** Location pages may need deeper technical SEO work (schema markup, citations) not just content rotation
3. **Lead Quality Mismatch:** Location pages may not be the right pages to rank for ("Charlotte, NC lawyer") vs. problem-solution pages ("motorcycle accident lawyer Charlotte NC")
4. **Budget Misallocation:** If paid ads are running to location pages with low CTR, budget may be wasted

---

## Root Cause Analysis: Why Location Pages Underperform

### Possible Reasons (Need Verification)

1. **Weak Content Quality**
   - Location pages may be too thin/generic
   - Lack unique value proposition per location
   - May not match search intent

2. **Poor Keyword Targeting**
   - Pages targeting "Charlotte, NC" alone (low intent)
   - Not targeting problem + location ("car accident lawyer Charlotte")
   - Missing local long-tail keywords with higher intent

3. **Technical SEO Issues**
   - Missing or incorrect schema markup
   - Location schema not properly configured
   - Citations not consistent across web
   - Local pack optimization missing

4. **Competitive Disadvantage**
   - Large national firms dominating local pack
   - Location pages can't compete without strong TrustRank signals
   - GMB may be underoptimized

5. **Search Intent Mismatch**
   - People searching "Charlotte, NC" + "personal injury lawyer" want practice area pages
   - People searching "motorcycle accident Charlotte" want specific service pages
   - Location pages are answering wrong question

### What We'd Need to Diagnose

- [ ] Current rankings for location pages (positions 1-100?)
- [ ] Impressions vs. clicks (are pages showing but getting no clicks?)
- [ ] Search queries triggering location pages (what are people searching?)
- [ ] Location page content audit (what's currently on them?)
- [ ] GMB status (reviews, posts, complete profile?)
- [ ] Citation consistency (are we listed everywhere correctly?)

## What Blog Content Does Right

### Why Blog/Resource Pages Excel

- **Higher Intent:** People actively searching for solutions = more clicks
- **Longer Content:** More ranking opportunities for multiple keywords
- **Educational Value:** People naturally link to helpful guides
- **Regular Updates:** Fresh content signals freshness to Google
- **Repurposing Potential:** Blog content can feed social, email, ads

### Blog/Resource Success Indicators

✅ Articles on specific injury types get traffic (e.g., "motorcycle accident fractures")  
✅ Educational guides get links  
✅ FAQ content addresses specific questions  
✅ Regular publishing signals active, helpful resource  
✅ Repurposing blog content for social drives brand awareness + authority

### Immediate (Weeks 1-4)

**Audit Blog Strategy:**

- [ ] Publishing frequency
- [ ] Topics that drive most traffic
- [ ] Topics with good conversion (leads generated)
- [ ] Republishing/updating opportunities

### Medium Term (Weeks 5-12)

**For Location Pages:**

If the problem is **content quality**, then:
- Rewrite location pages to match search intent
- Add problem-solving sections ("If you're injured in Charlotte, here's what to do")
- Add location-specific industry angles ("Manufacturing injuries in Charlotte")
- Add local testimonials/case results
- Improve conversion elements (CTAs, forms)

If the problem is **technical SEO**, then:
- Fix location schema markup
- Improve local citations (Google, Yelp, local directories)
- Enhance GMB with photos, posts, Q&A
- Build local backlinks
- Add location-specific structured data

If the problem is **keyword mismatch**, then:
- Target "injury lawyer + city" not just "city"
- Target "injury type + city" (motorcycle accident Charlotte)
- Add problem-solution keywords
- Create location-specific landing pages for high-intent searches

**For Blog Content:**

- Increase publishing frequency (current cadence?)
- Prioritize high-intent topics (injury types, recovery, legal process)
- Create topic clusters (main blog post + location variations)
- Optimize for featured snippets
- Build internal linking strategy (blog → practice areas → location pages)

### Content Calendar Revision

**Instead of current 50-week rotation (which dedicates 10 weeks to low-performing location pages):**

**Proposed 50-week rotation:**
- **Weeks 1-4:** Attorney spotlights (ongoing engagement)
- **Weeks 5-12:** Core practice areas (highest-value, highest-intent pages)
- **Weeks 13-24:** Blog content rotation (20% of calendar instead of current allocation)
- **Weeks 25-32:** Results/testimonials/awards (credibility signals)
- **Weeks 33-44:** Workers' comp deep dive + specialty content
- **Weeks 45-50:** Seasonal content, event coverage, special campaigns

**Location pages in this model:**
- Mentioned in practice area posts (link to Charlotte practice area page)
- Featured in GMB posts (not social rotation)
- Updated when significant news or case results for that area
- Optimized for GMB + local pack (not social driving traffic)

## The Real Play: Blog + Practice Areas + Location Pages as Support

### Traffic Hierarchy (Based on GSC Performance)

1. **Tier 1: Blog/Educational Content** (1.5-3.7%+ CTR)
   - Educational guides on injury types
   - Legal process explanations
   - Settlement/recovery timelines
   - Common questions answered
   
2. **Tier 2: Practice Area Pages** (Assumed strong based on industry norms)
   - Motorcycle accidents
   - Workers' compensation
   - Car accidents
   - Wrongful death
   
3. **Tier 3: Results/Testimonials** (Conversion support)
   - Case results showcase
   - Client testimonials
   - Awards/credentials
   
4. **Tier 4: Location Pages** (0.01-0.35% CTR)
   - Local community presence
   - Office information
   - GMB support
   - Not a primary traffic driver

### Where Social Media Fits

Your social strategy shouldn't try to fight Google's verdict. Instead:

- **Social drives brand awareness**, not organic search traffic (different channels)
- **Social amplifies blog content** (share articles → drive traffic + engagement)
- **Social supports local presence** (GMB posts, location-specific content)
- **Social builds authority** (establish expertise, not just rank)
- **Social adds credibility** (testimonials, case results, community)

**Location pages in social:** Mention occasionally, but don't create social posts expecting them to rank in search.
