## Approach A: Institutional Growth (Luana's Model)

### Core Strategy

Rotate through firm pages weekly to build brand awareness and GMB support. Focus on consistency, reach, and legal compliance.

### Content Structure

**50-week rotation of:**
- Attorney spotlights (4 weeks)
- Core practice areas (4 weeks)
- Results/testimonials (4 weeks)
- Vehicle accident details (4 weeks)
- Workers' comp specialty (10 weeks)
- Geographic pages (10 weeks)
- Educational/resources (10 weeks)

### Audience

- General North Carolina/South Carolina residents
- Injury-adjacent searches
- Broad awareness play

### KPIs

- Follower growth (primary)
- Engagement rate (secondary)
- Brand awareness metrics
- GMB profile visits

### Posting

- 1 post/week Facebook
- 1 post/week Instagram
- 1 post/week LinkedIn
- 2-3 posts/week Twitter
- Evergreen YouTube content

### Pros

✅ Lower execution risk (less personal)  
✅ Luana can manage independently  
✅ Compliant, corporate-safe messaging  
✅ Reaches broad audience  
✅ Supports GMB presence  

### Cons

❌ Competes with national firms on generic positioning  
❌ Ignores what GSC shows works (blog content)  
❌ Underutilizes Jason's authentic blue-collar positioning  
❌ Generic rotation = lower engagement  
❌ Doesn't show "cares about safety" angle  
❌ Misses competitive advantage (authenticity)

## Approach B: Authentic Blue-Collar Positioning (Website Synergy Model)

### Core Strategy

Amplify website's top-performing educational content through Jason's authentic personality. Position as "safety-conscious blue-collar lawyer" not "corporate firm."

### Content Structure

**70/30 split:**
- **70% Educational + Safety Focus:** Blog content, injury prevention, legal education, worker rights
- **30% Personality + Lifestyle:** Jason's motorcycles, cooking, shooting, cigars — real life, real person

**Monthly themes (rotating):**
- Week 1: Safety Education (from blog/practice areas)
- Week 2: Jason's World (motorcycles, lifestyle, authenticity)
- Week 3: Client Care (testimonials, case results, why he fights)
- Week 4: Blue-Collar Insights (worker rights, injury prevention, real talk)

### Audience

**Primary:** Blue-collar workers (construction, manufacturing, warehousing, trucking, healthcare)  
**Secondary:** Motorcycle community  
**Tertiary:** Small business owners managing worker safety  
**Emotional angle:** "Someone who gets your world and will fight for you"

### KPIs

- Lead quality (not just quantity)
- Blue-collar demographic reach
- Engagement on educational content
- Safety-focused message amplification
- Jason personal brand growth

### Posting

**Facebook:** 1-2 posts/week (longer form, education, stories)  
**Instagram:** 1-2 posts/week (@taylorserbinlaw for firm, Jason personal TBD)  
**LinkedIn:** 1 post/week (professional authority, blog amplification)  
**Twitter:** 2-3 posts/week (safety tips, real talk, news)  
**YouTube:** Educational series (injury prevention, legal process, case studies)

### The Synergy

1. **Website Blog** produces educational content
2. **Social amplifies** that content to blue-collar audience
3. **Jason's personality** makes education feel authentic ("this guy lives like us")
4. **Motorcycle community** sees Jason as insider (Rider's Life content)
5. **Workers** see safety education + lawyer who cares
6. **Safety focus** differentiates from "ambulance chaser" positioning
7. **Rough around edges** appeals to real workers (not corporate polish)

### Content Examples

**Educational (70%):**
- "5 signs your workers' comp claim is being denied unfairly"
- "Manufacturing injury? Here's the first 24 hours checklist"
- "Why you need a lawyer after a truck accident (not after you settle)"
- "PTSD after workplace injury? You have rights"
- "How to document your injury for maximum recovery"

**Personality (30%):**
- Jason on his motorcycle with rider safety tips
- Jason cooking a gourmet meal ("blue-collar chef energy")
- Jason at clay shooting ("safety = everything")
- Jason on motorcycle community events
- Jason's direct take on legal topics ("straight talk, no BS")

### Pros

✅ Differentiates from corporate competitors  
✅ Leverages Jason's authentic personality  
✅ Aligns with website's strongest content (blog)  
✅ Targets high-intent audience (blue-collar workers)  
✅ Supports "cares about safety" positioning  
✅ Higher engagement (authentic = shareable)  
✅ Personal brand builds Jason's thought leadership  
✅ GSC data shows educational content works  
✅ Rough around edges is a feature, not bug  
✅ Creates emotional connection ("he's one of us")

### Cons

❌ Requires more personal involvement from Jason  
❌ Requires consistent lifestyle content capture  
❌ Requires careful balance (not overly personal)  
❌ Higher execution complexity  
❌ Need to manage Jason's public presence carefully  
❌ Blue-collar positioning might not appeal to broader market

## The Winning Play: Hybrid

What if you did **Approach B for social media** (personality-driven, educational focus) + **Approach A for website** (page rotation driving GMB support)?

**The logic:**
- Social media builds brand awareness + personality (Approach B = higher engagement, authenticity)
- Website page rotation maintains institutional credibility + GMB support (Approach A = compliance, authority)
- Blog content is the bridge (written once, repurposed to social)
- Jason's personal brand on social drives traffic to website's educational content

**This means:**
- Social = Jason + blue-collar positioning + education + lifestyle
- Website = Institutional credibility + practice area authority + office presence
- Blog = Educational content (shared resource)
- Both work together (not in competition)

## Recommendation Framework

**Choose Approach A if:**
- Jason wants low personal visibility
- Firm wants purely institutional positioning
- Content creation should be entirely Luana-managed
- Broad reach > lead quality

**Choose Approach B if:**
- Jason enjoys personal brand building
- Firm values authenticity + differentiation
- Blue-collar market is priority
- Lead quality > broad reach
- You can commit to lifestyle content capture

**Choose Hybrid if:**
- You want both reach and authenticity
- Jason willing to be front-and-center on social (not website)
- Resources allow both approaches in parallel
- Want to use website for credibility, social for personality
