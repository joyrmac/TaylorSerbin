## Strategic Overview

Jason's personal brand serves as a **strategic bridge** between the firm's corporate identity (broad, professional) and the motorcycle community (loyal, high-value client source).

**The Opportunity:**
- Firm moved away from motorcycle-centric branding 2 years ago to diversify
- Motorcycle community represents deep loyalty and legacy clients
- Jason is authentically part of this community—not marketing to it
- Personal brand maintains the connection without diluting firm's broader positioning

- **Logo:** Cigar-smoking alligator on a motorcycle
- **Positioning:** Tough, experienced, no-nonsense. Battle-tested trial attorney who is also a committed rider

Jason isn't marketing to these communities—he IS these communities:
- **Actual rider:** Owns and rides motorcycles regularly
- **Actual gourmet cook:** Passionate about cooking (escargot, etc.)
- **Actual cigar smoker:** Genuine collector and enthusiast
- **Actual outdoorsman:** Five-stand clay shooting participant
- **Actual trial attorney:** Battle-tested in litigation

**Non-negotiable:** No faking. Every piece of content is rooted in real Jason.

## Content Pillars

### 1. The Rider's Life (40% of content)
**What:** Authentic footage and stories from Jason's motorcycle life

**Examples:**
- Riding updates and road shots
- Motorcycle maintenance/tips for riders
- Regional bike events (rallies, toy runs, meet-ups)
- Jason attending/sponsoring rider events
- Bike culture moments

**Platform:** Instagram (primary), Facebook (secondary)

**Tone:** Raw, authentic, unpolished. High-quality camera, low production

**What:** Behind-the-scenes cooking content + recipes

**Examples:**
- Monthly featured recipes (e.g., his escargot)
- Kitchen prep and cooking process
- Plating/finished dishes
- Food philosophy/sourcing
- Entertaining/hosting moments

**Platform:** Instagram (Reels + static), Facebook

**Tone:** Enthusiastic, expert, accessible. Show the craft, not the performance

**What:** Cigar collecting, shooting sports, lifestyle hobbies

- Cigar reviews/recommendations

- Lifestyle moments

**Platform:** Instagram, LinkedIn (position as success/balance)

**Tone:** Knowledgeable, relaxed, peer-to-peer

**Examples:**
- "Did you know?" legal facts

## Platform Strategy

### Instagram (@Jasontaylorthelitigator)
**Primary platform for personal brand**

**Content Mix:**
- 40% Rider's Life (photos, short videos, Reels)
- 20% Gourmet Litigator (photos, Reels)
- 20% Cigars & Five-Stand (photos, stories)
- 20% Alligator Wisdom (carousel posts, static)

**Posting Cadence:** 4–5 posts/week (consistent, not overwhelming)

**Engagement:** Respond to comments, share rider/cigar/cooking community content, build peer-to-peer relationships

**Link Strategy:** Link to firm website in bio; mention in captions only when relevant (no hard selling)

### LinkedIn
**Secondary platform. Professional credibility + business reach**

**Content Mix:**
- 50% Alligator Wisdom (legal insights, thought leadership)
- 30% Rider's Life (professional identity + authenticity angle)
- 20% Balance/lifestyle (cooking, shooting, travel)

**Posting Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week

**Engagement:** Legal community, referral sources, potential business clients

**Tone:** Professional but honest. "Here's how I stay sharp, here's how I help clients"

### Facebook (Jason E. Taylor, The Litigator)
**Tertiary platform. Community + past clients**

**Content Mix:**
- Mirror Instagram (Rider's Life, cooking, events)
- Community announcements (toy runs, sponsorships, events)
- Client testimonials/case wins
- Longer-form storytelling

**Posting Cadence:** 3–4 posts/week

**Engagement:** Community building, event announcements, past client connection

## Content Operations

### Raw Feed Model (Low Friction)
**Jason's role:**
- Capture photos/videos in the moment (phone is fine)
- Send to agency weekly or as events happen
- Provide context: "Riding in Asheville," "Made escargot last night," etc.

**Agency role:**
- Edit and enhance (if needed)
- Write captions
- Format for each platform
- Schedule posting
- Monitor engagement and respond

**Advantage:** Jason focuses on living; agency handles execution pain

### Onboarding Content
**Before launch, capture:**
- 10–15 photos of Jason with motorcycles (various settings, bikes, seasons)
- 5 cooking/kitchen photos (prep, finished dishes, process)
- 5 cigar/shooting photos
- 5 candid lifestyle photos
- 3–5 video clips of Jason talking (casual, unscripted)

**This gives agency 2–3 weeks of content buffer before real-time operations begin.**

## Success Metrics

### Engagement (Primary)
- Comments and shares from motorcycle/local communities
- Follower growth (should indicate resonance, not vanity)
- Save rate on posts (indicates actual value)
- Share rate (people recommending Jason to friends)

### Lead Quality (Secondary)
- Motorcycle-related inquiries traced to personal brand
- Quality of inbound messages (serious vs. spam)
- Conversion from follower → consultation
- Referrals from motorcycle community

### Authority (Tertiary)
- Mentions in motorcycle community (online, clubs, forums)
- Speaking invitations or partnership opportunities
- Brand sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)
- LinkedIn engagement from legal peers

### NOT Tracking
- Vanity metrics (total followers, impressions)
- Likes (low-signal engagement)
- Reach (volume without intent)

## Brand Guardrails

**What We DO:**
- Post authentically about Jason's real life
- Show expertise without preaching
- Build peer relationships, not follower lists
- Link back to firm value without hard selling
- Respect the motorcycle community (don't use them for marketing)

**What We DON'T:**
- Manufacture content that isn't real
- Oversell the legal services ("Call now!")
- Post trends Jason doesn't care about
- Compete with influencer culture
- Dilute the "human first, attorney second" positioning

**The Alligator Logo Test:**
If it feels like "content marketing," we don't post it. If it feels like Jason sharing his life, we do.

## Integration with Firm Website

**Personal brand supports the firm, not the reverse.**

- Every post can subtly mention firm values (Strong Advocate, Client First)
- Links to website are organic, not forced
- Personal brand builds trust; firm website converts to clients
- Jason's credibility elevates firm authority
- Personal brand owns motorcycle community; firm owns broader practice

**Example:** A post about motorcycle safety tips can end with "If you're injured, call [firm]" — but only if it serves the reader first.
