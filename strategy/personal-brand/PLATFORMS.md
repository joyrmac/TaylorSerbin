## INSTAGRAM (@Jasontaylorthelitigator)

### Role
**Primary platform** for Jason's personal brand. The main stage.

### Audience
- Motorcycle community (primary)
- High-net-worth professionals
- People researching Jason before hiring
- Current clients and past cases

### Content Mix
- 40% Rider's Life (photos, Reels, Stories)
- 20% Gourmet Litigator (photos, Reels)
- 20% Cigars & Five-Stand (photos, Stories)
- 20% Alligator Wisdom (carousel posts, static)

### Post Types & Strategy

**Rider's Life (Weekly: 2 posts)**
- **Type:** Photo + carousel
- **Examples:** Bike shot, riding update, event attendance, gear/maintenance
- **Length:** Caption 50–100 words
- **Tone:** Raw, authentic, in-the-moment feeling
- **CTA:** Optional ("What bike do you ride?" or just a moment shared)

**Gourmet Litigator (Every 2 weeks: 1 post)**
- **Type:** Photo + Reel
- **Examples:** Finished dish, prep process, hosting moment
- **Length:** Caption 75–150 words (storytelling okay here)
- **Tone:** Enthusiastic, expert, accessible
- **CTA:** Optional ("Recipe incoming?" or ask followers)

**Cigars & Five-Stand (Weekly: 1 post)**
- **Type:** Photo + Story
- **Examples:** Cigar review, shooting moment, event update
- **Length:** Caption 50–100 words
- **Tone:** Knowledgeable, relaxed, peer-to-peer
- **CTA:** Recommendation or question for community

**Alligator Wisdom (Weekly: 1 post)**
- **Type:** Carousel (3–5 slides) or static with text overlay
- **Examples:** "Did you know?" legal fact, insurance tip, safety advice
- **Length:** 100–150 words per slide (make it readable)
- **Tone:** Direct, expert, educational
- **CTA:** "Save this for later" or "Ask in comments"
- **Visual:** Alligator logo should appear

### Posting Cadence
- **4–5 posts per week** (consistent, not overwhelming)
- **Optimal posting times:** Wed–Sat (when community is most active)
- **Avoid:** Sunday–Monday typically slower

### Engagement Strategy
- Respond to all comments within 24 hours
- Reply as Jason, not corporate
- Engage with motorcycle/cigar/cooking community content (not just your own)
- Share others' content to Stories (give shout-outs)
- Never engage with spam or off-brand content
- Monitor for DM inquiries (may be potential clients)

### Stories Strategy
- Use Stories for real-time moments (behind-the-scenes, daily life)
- 3–5 Stories per week minimum
- Ephemeral = more authentic, less pressure to be perfect
- Highlight key posts (Alligator Wisdom, major announcements)

### Reels Strategy
- Reels get 3x more reach than static posts
- 1 Reel per week minimum
- Ideas: Cooking process, riding moment, cigar unboxing, quick legal tip
- Keep under 60 seconds
- Use captions/text overlays (accessibility + watchable on mute)

### Hashtag Strategy
- 3–5 relevant hashtags per post (no hashtag spam)
- Mix broad (#motorcycles, #cigarlife) and niche (#NCriders, #carolinamotorcycles)
- Research trending hashtags in cycling/cigar communities
- Never use generic like #amazing or #instagood
- Rotate hashtags (don't use same 5 every time)

## LINKEDIN

### Role
**Secondary platform** for professional credibility + business reach. The authority stage.

### Audience
- Legal professionals and colleagues
- Referral sources (other attorneys, insurance adjusters)
- Potential business clients
- Professional network

### Content Mix
- 50% Alligator Wisdom (legal insights, thought leadership)
- 30% Rider's Life (professional identity + authenticity angle)
- 20% Balance/lifestyle (cooking, shooting, travel—show the person)

### Post Types & Strategy

**Alligator Wisdom (2–3 posts per week)**
- **Type:** Text post or carousel with visual
- **Topic:** Legal insights, industry trends, safety tips, regulatory changes
- **Length:** 150–300 words (LinkedIn favors longer-form)
- **Tone:** Professional but honest. Educate, don't promote.
- **Example:** "Here's what you should know about motorcycle insurance in NC..."
- **CTA:** "What questions do you have?" or "Save this for reference"

**Rider's Life (1–2 posts per week)**
- **Type:** Photo + text
- **Topic:** Riding moments, trips, community events, authenticity
- **Length:** 100–150 words
- **Tone:** Professional + personal. Show work-life balance.
- **Example:** "How I stay sharp: weekend rides + trial prep + escargot"
- **CTA:** Optional. Focus on the story.

**Balance/Lifestyle (0–1 posts per week)**
- **Type:** Photo + reflection
- **Topic:** Cooking, shooting, travel—humanize the attorney
- **Length:** 75–150 words
- **Tone:** Reflective, warm, professional
- **Example:** "Sunday's clay shooting competition reminds me why trial prep matters..."
- **CTA:** "What helps you reset?"

### Posting Cadence
- **2–3 posts per week** (consistent, not overwhelming)
- **Optimal posting times:** Tue–Thu (weekday professional browsing)
- **Best length:** Tuesday–Wednesday mornings

### Engagement Strategy
- Respond to comments (LinkedIn favors engagement)
- Reply as Jason, thoughtfully
- Engage with legal community content
- Share other attorneys' insights
- Connect with referral sources and colleagues
- Use LinkedIn newsletter if building email list

### Article Strategy
- **Long-form articles** (500–1500 words) drive authority
- 1 article per month on a legal topic
- Examples: "5 Mistakes Motorcycle Accident Victims Make" or "Understanding Your NC Insurance"
- Repurpose blog content from firm website
- Include Alligator Wisdom logo/branding

## FACEBOOK

### Role
**Tertiary platform** for community + past clients. The community stage.

### Audience
- Community members and past clients
- Local motorcycle clubs and groups
- Followers of firm page (overlap)
- Older demographic (LinkedIn + Instagram skew younger)

### Content Mix
- Mirror Instagram (Rider's Life 40%, Gourmet 20%, Cigars 20%, Wisdom 20%)
- Add community announcements (toy runs, events, sponsorships)
- Add client testimonials or wins (with permission)
- Longer-form storytelling (Facebook allows it)

### Post Types & Strategy

**Everything from Instagram**
- Cross-post Instagram content to Facebook
- Add Facebook-specific longer captions (up to 500 words)
- Encourage discussion in comments
- Tag people and places (Facebook algorithm favors this)

**Community Announcements**
- Type: Photo + text
- Examples: "Toy run this Saturday," "Sponsoring the local bike rally"
- Length: 150–250 words
- CTA: "Register here" or "Who's coming?"

**Client Testimonials/Wins**
- Type: Quote graphic or video testimonial
- With permission only
- Anonymize case details
- Focus on outcome + client satisfaction
- Length: 100–200 words

**Longer Stories**
- Facebook is more tolerant of long-form
- Can tell richer stories (riding trips, cooking experiences)
- Add multiple photos (carousel)
- Use line breaks and formatting for readability

### Posting Cadence
- **3–4 posts per week** (okay to mirror Instagram with local additions)
- **Optimal times:** Evenings and weekends (community browsing)

### Engagement Strategy
- Respond to comments (Facebook algorithm favors active pages)
- Share/tag local businesses and community members
- Create community events (Facebook Events feature)
- Use Facebook Groups if there's a community angle
- Pin important posts (announcements, events)

## CROSS-PLATFORM STRATEGY

### What Gets Posted Where

| Content | Instagram | LinkedIn | Facebook |
|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Rider's Life (casual moment) | Yes | Rare | Yes |
| Rider's Life (interesting story) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Cooking moment | Yes | No | Yes |
| Cigar review | Yes | No | Yes |
| Shooting update | Yes | No | Yes |
| Legal tip (Alligator) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Case win (anonymized) | No | Maybe | Yes |
| Community event | Yes | No | Yes |
| Firm announcement | No | Maybe | Yes |

### Posting Process

1. **Create content** (photo/video/caption)
2. **Format for Instagram first** (primary platform rules apply)
3. **Adapt caption for LinkedIn** (if Alligator Wisdom or professional angle)
4. **Cross-post to Facebook** (longer caption, more community focus)
5. **Schedule simultaneously** (or stagger by 1–2 hours if testing)

### Platform-Specific Rules

**Instagram:**
- Captions under 150 words (readability)
- Hashtags essential (3–5)
- Emojis okay if authentic
- Stories for daily moments
- Reels for algorithm boost

**LinkedIn:**
- Captions can be 150–300+ words
- Hashtags useful but less critical
- Professional tone always
- Avoid emojis (unless very rare)
- Long-form articles for authority

**Facebook:**
- Longest captions (400–500 words okay)
- Tags and location boost reach
- Community-first tone
- Emojis acceptable
- Events and groups for community building
