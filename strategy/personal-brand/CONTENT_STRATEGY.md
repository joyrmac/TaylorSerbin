## Strategic Overview

Jason's personal brand serves as the **bridge between the firm's corporate identity and the motorcycle community**.

### The Situation
- Two years ago, Taylor | Serbin shifted away from motorcycle-centric branding to diversify practice areas
- The motorcycle community represents deep loyalty and high-value clients
- Jason is authentically part of this community
- **Solution:** Jason's personal brand maintains that connection without diluting firm's broader positioning

### The Goal
**Humanize the firm by leading with Jason's authentic passion for the motorcycle community. Build high-authority personal brand that connects peer-to-peer, showing the "human behind the suit" before legal need arises.**

## Brand Identity

### The Litigator
- **Persona:** Cigar-smoking alligator on a motorcycle
- **Positioning:** Tough, experienced, no-nonsense. Battle-tested trial attorney who is also a committed rider
- **Voice:** Direct, gritty, honest. Like a conversation over a cigar—expert but accessible

### Core Differentiator: AUTHENTICITY
**Jason isn't marketing TO these communities—he IS these communities.**

- Actual rider (not an attorney who dabbles)
- Actual gourmet cook (not catering-ordered meals)
- Actual cigar collector (not corporate gifts)
- Actual outdoorsman (participates in five-stand shooting)
- Actual trial attorney (battle-tested in litigation)

**Non-negotiable:** No fake content. Every post is rooted in Jason's real life.

### Pillar 1: The Rider's Life (40% of Content)
**What:** Authentic footage of Jason on his bike and at regional events

**Why:** Primary audience is motorcycle community. This is where Jason is most authentically himself.

## Platform Strategy

### Instagram (@Jasontaylorthelitigator)
**Primary Platform. The main stage.**

**Content Mix:**
- 40% Rider's Life (photos, short videos, Reels)
- 20% Gourmet Litigator (photos, Reels)
- 20% Cigars & Five-Stand (photos, Stories)
- 20% Alligator Wisdom (carousel posts, static)

**Posting Cadence:** 4–5 posts/week

**Stories:** 3–5 per week (real-time, behind-the-scenes)

**Reels:** 1 per week minimum (algorithm boost)

**Engagement:** Respond to comments within 24 hours. Be human. Build peer relationships.

**Link Strategy:** Link to firm website in bio. Mention firm in captions only when organic.

---

### LinkedIn
**Secondary Platform. Professional credibility + business reach.**

**Content Mix:**
- 50% Alligator Wisdom (legal insights, thought leadership)
- 30% Rider's Life (professional identity + authenticity)
- 20% Balance/lifestyle (cooking, shooting, travel)

**Posting Cadence:** 2–3 posts/week

**Articles:** 1 per month (long-form, 500–1500 words)

**Engagement:** Legal community, referral sources, potential business clients.

**Tone:** Professional but honest. Show full person. "Here's how I stay sharp, here's how I help clients."

---

### Facebook (Jason E. Taylor, The Litigator)
**Tertiary Platform. Community + past clients.**

**Content Mix:**
- Mirror Instagram (Rider's Life, cooking, cigars, wisdom)
- Community announcements (toy runs, sponsorships, events)
- Client testimonials/case wins (with permission)
- Longer-form storytelling

**Posting Cadence:** 3–4 posts/week

**Engagement:** Community building, event announcements, past client connection.

**Tone:** Community-first. Mix of professional and casual.

## Content Operations Model

### The "Raw Feed" System
**Jason's Role (Low Friction):**
- Capture photos and videos in the moment (phone is fine)
- Send to agency weekly or as events happen
- Provide context: "Riding in Asheville," "Made escargot last night," etc.
- Approve tone/voice of first posts

**Agency's Role (Handle the Work):**
- Organize and sort content by pillar
- Edit and enhance (if needed)
- Write captions in Jason's voice
- Format for each platform
- Schedule posting
- Monitor engagement and respond

**Advantage:** Jason focuses on living; agency handles execution pain.

## Success Metrics

### Primary: Engagement Quality
- Comments and shares from motorcycle/local communities
- Follower growth indicating genuine resonance
- Save rate on posts (actual value indicator)
- Share rate (people recommending Jason to friends)

### Secondary: Lead Quality
- Motorcycle-related inquiries traced to personal brand
- Quality of inbound messages (serious vs. spam)
- Conversion from follower → consultation
- Referrals from motorcycle community

### Tertiary: Authority
- Mentions in motorcycle community (online, clubs, forums)
- Speaking invitations or partnership opportunities
- Brand sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)
- LinkedIn engagement from legal peers

### NOT Tracking
- Vanity metrics (total followers, impressions)
- Likes (low-signal engagement)
- Reach (volume without intent)

## Integration with Firm Website

### The Connection (Not Competition)

**Personal brand supports the firm, not the reverse.**

- Every post can subtly mention firm values (Strong Advocate, Client First)
- Links to website are organic, not forced
- Personal brand builds trust; firm website converts to clients
- Jason's credibility elevates firm authority
- Personal brand owns motorcycle community; firm owns broader practice

**Example:** A post about motorcycle safety tips can end with "If you're injured, call [firm]" — but only if it serves the reader first.

### Avoiding Dilution
- Personal brand is Jason's lifestyle + expertise
- Firm website is the firm's authority + services
- They reinforce each other without competing

---

## Brand Guardrails

### What We DO
- Post authentically about Jason's real life
- Show expertise without preaching
- Build peer relationships, not follower lists
- Link back to firm value without hard selling
- Respect the motorcycle community (don't use them for marketing)

### What We DON'T
- Manufacture content that isn't real
- Oversell legal services ("Call now!")
- Post trends Jason doesn't care about
- Compete with influencer culture
- Dilute the "human first, attorney second" positioning

### The Alligator Logo Test
**If it feels like "content marketing," we don't post it.**  
**If it feels like Jason sharing his life, we do.**
