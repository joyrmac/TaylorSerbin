**Date:** February 5, 2026  
**Source:** Google Search Console data + Doctrine audit

---

## Executive Summary

**The Problem:** thelitigator.com generates 500k+ monthly search impressions but converts only ~3,700 clicks across top 30 pages. Location page strategy violates the operating doctrine and produces near-zero CTR.

**The Root Cause:** Site structure prioritizes SEO volume over user intent. Hundreds of location variations, near-duplicate content, and location-page-first design drive impressions without conversion.

**The Opportunity:** High-intent blog content (grass clippings, medpay, traffic cameras) achieves 3-4x better CTR. Users want answers, not location sprawl.

---

## Performance Reality (Current State)

### What's Working
- **Blog posts answering real questions:** 1.5–3.7% CTR
  - "Is blowing grass clippings illegal?" — 3.68% CTR (453 clicks from 12,326 impressions)
  - "Charlotte traffic cameras" — 1.57% CTR (537 clicks from 34,292 impressions)
  - "How does medpay work?" — 1.77% CTR (295 clicks from 16,701 impressions)

- **Homepage:** 0.59% CTR (not great, but baseline)

### What's Failing
- **Location pages:** 0.01–0.35% CTR
  - Charlotte, NC (411k impressions) = 457 clicks (0.11% CTR)
  - Rock Hill, SC (191k impressions) = 21 clicks (0.01% CTR)
  - Hickory, NC (112k impressions) = 400 clicks (0.35% CTR)

- **Attorney pages:** 0.88% CTR average
  - Jason Taylor page (0.88% CTR) — 82 clicks from 9,274 impressions
  - Firm team pages underperform vs. blog content

- **Location + practice area combinations:** Often 0 clicks despite ranking well

### The Math
- Location pages: ~650k combined impressions, ~900 clicks (0.14% CTR)
- High-intent blogs: ~75k combined impressions, ~1,300 clicks (1.73% CTR)
- **Conclusion:** Blog content converts 12x better than location pages

## Doctrine Violations

**Operating Manual Rule:** "If something does not improve understanding, authority, or outcomes, it does not belong here."

**What's violating the doctrine:**

1. **Aspirational Geography** (SEO_LOCATION_STRATEGY violation)
   - Location pages suggest physical presence and service where reality may vary
   - No single, clear geographic frame per page
   - Multiple city variations dilute authority

2. **Content for Content's Sake** (EXECUTION_STANDARDS violation)
   - Location/practice area matrix generates pages with zero clicks
   - Effort is not improving outcomes
   - Structure serves SEO mechanics, not user intent

3. **SEO-First Design** (BRAND_AUTHORITY violation)
   - Site structure optimized for keyword coverage, not clarity
   - Users see too many location options and pick none (analysis: low CTR)
   - Authority is diluted across hundreds of similar pages

4. **Over-Optimization** (SEO_LOCATION_STRATEGY violation)
   - Metadata, headings stacked with keywords
   - Ranking well (positions 4–5) but not clicking through (0.01% CTR)
   - Classic sign of search-gaming that doesn't serve users

## Decision Point

**Before moving forward, leadership must decide:**

**Option A: Minimize Location Pages**
- Keep only pages for offices with real presence (Charlotte, Hickory, Columbia?)
- Reframe as "Practice Areas" + "Locations We Serve" instead of city sprawl
- Risk: Lose impressions from long-tail location searches
- Benefit: Improve CTR, authority, and conversion

**Option B: Restructure Location Pages**
- Keep location pages but rewrite for user intent (not SEO volume)
- Lead with content answer, then offer office info
- Consolidate similar cities
- Risk: Still may underperform vs. high-intent content
- Benefit: Preserves some search visibility

**Option C: Status Quo**
- Keep current structure
- Acknowledge low CTR is acceptable for impression volume
- Risk: Violates operating doctrine
- Benefit: None

**Recommendation:** **Option A** — aligns with doctrine, fixes conversion, builds authority
