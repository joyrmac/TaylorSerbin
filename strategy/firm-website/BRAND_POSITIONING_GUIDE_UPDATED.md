## FIRM BRAND VOICE & TONE

### Core Characteristics

**1. Direct & Gritty**
- Straight talk, no corporate fluff
- "This is what happened. Here's what we do about it."
- Conversational but professional
- *Language:* "We fight for injured people. That's all we do."

**2. Trial-Ready & Confident**
- Emphasis on preparation and courtroom readiness
- Willingness to fight, not just settle
- Demonstrated experience
- *Language:* "Insurance companies count on people giving up. We don't."

**3. Honest & Transparent**
- Realistic about timelines and outcomes
- Clear about process and fees
- No overpromising
- *Language:* "Here's what I can do for you. Here's what this will take."

**4. Human-Centered & Compassionate**
- Understanding that injury = life disruption
- Focus on recovery and support
- People-first approach
- *Language:* "You're not a case number. You're a person with a life to rebuild."

**5. Local & Accessible**
- Carolinas-specific (not national firm energy)
- Community presence and relationships
- Approachable, not intimidating
- *Language:* "We know the Carolinas. We know what works here."

### What To Say (Firm Brand)

✅ **DO:**
- "Here's what happened to you, and here's what we're going to do"
- "We prepare cases for trial. Insurance companies know it. They negotiate differently."
- "You're not a case number. You're a person with a life to rebuild."
- "We've been through this. You haven't. Let's talk."
- "Trial-ready. Always."
- "Settlements and verdicts from $70,000 to $4.2M+"
- "Multiple offices across the Carolinas. One coordinated team."

❌ **DON'T:**
- Promise specific settlement amounts
- Use fear-mongering ("Insurance companies are evil!")
- Sound like national firm ("We fight across America!")
- Overpromise results
- Use aggressive "ambulance chaser" energy
- Sound corporate or formal
- Make unverified claims about total recovered
