## SOCIAL MEDIA GEOGRAPHIC STRATEGY

### Geographic Specificity Rule

**Requirement:** Every post should include geographic relevance where possible.

**Types of Geographic Posts:**

**Type 1: Location-Specific Posts** (Charlotte, Rock Hill, etc.)
- Frame: "If you were injured in [Location]..."
- Content: Location-specific practice area + local context
- Frequency: Rotate through primary offices (Charlotte 6-8 weeks, Rock Hill 8-10 weeks, Concord 8-10 weeks, Hickory 10-12 weeks)
- Example: "Charlotte motorcycle accident? Here's what NC law says about your rights..."

**Type 2: Region-Specific Posts** (North Carolina vs. South Carolina)
- Frame: "North Carolina workplace injury" or "South Carolina wrongful death"
- Content: State-specific legal requirements + practice area
- Frequency: Mixed throughout month
- Example: "North Carolina workers' compensation laws changed. Here's what that means for your case..."

**Type 3: Regional/Broad Posts** ("The Carolinas")
- Frame: "Injured in the Carolinas?"
- Content: Broad positioning + firm's multi-location accessibility
- Frequency: Regular mix with location-specific posts
- Example: "Motorcycle accident anywhere in the Carolinas? We know motorcycle law. We know motorcycle community."

**Type 4: Industry-Specific Posts** (Local industries by region)
- Frame: "Manufacturing injury in Hickory?" or "Warehouse accident in Rock Hill?"
- Content: Industry-specific hazards + legal rights
- Frequency: Seasonal and incident-responsive
- Example: "Construction accidents in the Charlotte area are different. Here's what you need to know..."
