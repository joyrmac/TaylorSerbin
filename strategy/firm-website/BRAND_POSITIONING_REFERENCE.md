## TONE & VOICE ANALYSIS

### Tone
- Professional but accessible
- Direct and confident
- Authority-based but not aloof
- Results-oriented

### Key Phrases/Language
- "Trial lawyer first approach"
- "Coordinated legal team"
- "We encourage informed decisions"
- "Your rights" (emphasis on client agency)
- "From day one" (commitment emphasis)

### What's NOT Present
- Aggressive sales language
- Hard sells or urgency language
- "Don't delay" type messaging
- Overly casual tone
- "We'll fight for you" type clichés

## DIFFERENTIATION ELEMENTS

### vs. Other Injury Firms
- Emphasis on being a **trial-ready firm** (not just settlement negotiators)
- **Coordinated team approach** (collaborative, not one-attorney shop)
- **Geographic specificity** (NC & SC multi-office presence)
- **Multiple practice areas** (not pigeonholed into one injury type)

### Jason's Personal Brand Implications
The firm website positions as:
- Professional, broad-based, institutional
- Multiple attorneys and locations
- Trial-focused and authority-based
- Serious/formal tone

**Jason's personal brand should be:**
- Individual and authentic
- Lifestyle + expertise blend
- Approachable and peer-level tone
- Personality-driven (motorcycle, cooking, cigars, shooting)
- Community-focused (motorcycle groups, local engagement)

This creates a *complementary* dual-track strategy rather than competition.
