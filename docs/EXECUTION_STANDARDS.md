# Execution Standards
**Source:** Operating Manual, Version 1.0

---

## Tone Rules

**All output must be:**
- Direct
- Grounded
- Declarative
- Calmly confident

**We do NOT use:**
- Hype language
- Marketing fluff
- Emojis in professional content (personal brand is different)
- Excessive qualifiers
- "Thought leadership" phrasing

---

## Length Discipline

**Default rule:** Shorter is better until meaning is lost

- Say the thing once
- No repeating the same idea in different words
- No padding for SEO or aesthetics
- No sections added "just in case"

If content gets longer, it must get *clearer* at the same time.

---

## CTA Rules

**Every public-facing asset must have:**
- One clear primary CTA
- Language tied to user intent, not ego

**We do NOT:**
- Stack CTAs
- Use vague CTAs ("Learn more" without context)
- Add CTAs that don't match the content

**Truth:** If a CTA feels forced, the content is wrong—not the CTA.

---

## Publishing Threshold

**Work is publishable when:**
- The structure is stable
- The message is unambiguous
- The content can stand alone without explanation

**Work is NOT publishable when:**
- Changes are purely cosmetic
- Revisions are driven by anxiety
- You are "just checking one more thing"

**Truth:** Finished is a decision, not a feeling.

---

## Visual Usage Rules

**Visuals must:**
- Reinforce the message
- Match the established aesthetic system
- Be consistent across related assets

**Visuals must NOT:**
- Decorate weak content
- Introduce ambiguity
- Compete with the message
- Exist without a reason

**Test:** If the content works without the visual, the visual must earn its place.

---

## Revision Rules

**Revisions are allowed when they:**
- Improve clarity
- Correct inaccuracies
- Strengthen structure

**Revisions are stopped when they:**
- Change tone without purpose
- Add words without meaning
- Exist to soothe uncertainty

**Truth:** More passes do not equal better work.

---

## Authority Protection

**We do NOT publish content that:**
- Overpromises
- Sounds unsure
- Tries to impress peers instead of serve users
- Requires justification after release

**Standard:** Quiet authority is the standard.

---

## For Social Media Specifically

### Instagram / Facebook / LinkedIn

**Tone:**
- Authentic before polished
- Personality before corporate
- Human before brand

**Captions:**
- Start with the point
- Keep under 150 words (exception: storytelling posts)
- One clear CTA or observation (not both)
- No hashtag spam (3–5 relevant hashtags max)

**Frequency:**
- Instagram: 4–5 posts/week
- LinkedIn: 2–3 posts/week
- Facebook: 3–4 posts/week
- Better to miss a day than post filler

**Engagement:**
- Reply to comments within 24 hours
- Be human in responses (no canned replies)
- Engage with community content (not just your own)
- Don't engage with spam or off-brand content

**Never:**
- Post while angry or emotional
- Use engagement bait ("Like if you agree!")
- Copy competitor posts
- Post for the sake of posting
- Respond defensively to criticism
