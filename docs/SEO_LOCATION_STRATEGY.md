# SEO & Location Rules: Taylor | Serbin
**Client:** Taylor | Serbin Injury Lawyers  
**Source:** Operating Manual, Version 1.0

---

## SEO Intent

**SEO for Taylor | Serbin exists to:**
- Clarify who the firm is
- Clarify where the firm practices
- Reinforce trial-level credibility
- Support long-term authority, not short-term traffic spikes

**Rule:** SEO is never used to manufacture reach.

---

## Firm Entity Rules

**Primary entity:** Taylor | Serbin Injury Lawyers

**Entity usage must:**
- Be consistent across the site
- Appear naturally
- Reinforce authority, not branding theatrics

**We do NOT:**
- Repeat the firm name excessively
- Force entity mentions into headings
- Use keyword-driven variations of the firm name

---

## Attorney Entity Usage

**Attorney names are used:**
- When contextually relevant
- When credibility or responsibility matters
- When tied to real outcomes or roles

**We do NOT:**
- Name-drop attorneys for padding
- Stack bios or credentials inside content pages
- Use attorney names to "SEO juice" pages

**Principle:** Authority is assumed, not advertised.

---

## Location Reality

**Taylor | Serbin serves clearly defined geographic markets through real offices and real service areas.**

**Location references must:**
- Reflect reality exactly
- Match the Contact page and footer
- Be consistent across site, captions, and metadata

**Rule:** No aspirational geography is allowed.

---

## Primary vs. Secondary Locations

**Rules:**
- One primary geographic framing per page
- Secondary locations only when explicitly justified
- Service-area language must match how the firm actually operates

**We do NOT:**
- Treat all cities as equal
- Create artificial "coverage" language
- Suggest physical presence where none exists

---

## Practice Area Localization

**Practice area pages:**
- May include geographic context
- Must not read like location pages
- Should prioritize clarity of legal process over geo signals

**Test:** If location overwhelms substance, the page is wrong.

---

## Captions, Overlays, and Supporting Content

**Rules:**
- Location mentions must feel natural
- One geographic reference is usually enough
- No city lists
- No SEO-style phrasing

**Test:** If a location callout feels noticeable, it is already too much.

---

## Metadata Discipline

**Titles and descriptions must:**
- Reflect the actual page content
- Avoid keyword-stacked phrasing
- Match the seriousness of the brand

**Standards:**
- No hype
- No urgency manipulation
- No marketing tricks

---

## Disallowed SEO Behaviors

**Taylor | Serbin explicitly does NOT use:**
- Near-duplicate pages
- Swapped-city templates
- Overwritten H1s
- "Serving X, Y, Z" padding
- SEO-first copywriting

**Rule:** If it looks like SEO, it probably does not belong.

---

## Validation Check (Before Publishing Any Location Content)

**Before publishing:**
- Does this reflect reality?
- Would this confuse a real client?
- Would this still feel correct in two years?

**Action:** If not, revise.

---

## Current State (From Performance Audit)

**ISSUE:** Location pages are generating 650k+ impressions but only ~900 clicks (0.14% CTR) due to:
- Over-proliferation of location variations
- Low-quality content
- Users seeing too many options and picking none
- Violation of "one primary geographic frame" rule

**DECISION PENDING:** Consolidate vs. restructure vs. eliminate location page strategy

See `/strategy/firm-website/CRITICAL_ISSUES.md` for full analysis.
